    Pakoras

    Ingredients

  • 250 grams of chickpea flour
  • half a teaspoon of baking powder
  • a teaspoon of chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 green chilli pepper
  • pinch of ground turmeric
  • an onion
  • vegetable oil
  • salt

Put the flour, baking powder, chilli powder, coriander, chilli, tumeric and chopped onion in a large bowl. Season with salt and mix together with enough water to make a thick batter.

Heat enough oil for deep frying in a kadhai or deep, heavy-based pan to 180Â°C or until a cube of bread browns in 30 seconds. Working in batches, drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil and deep-fry for 3-4 minutes, or until golden brown. Remove from the heat with a slotted spoon and drain on crumpled up paper towels. Serve plain.

    Aloo Baigan Sookha

    Ingredients

  • 3 tablepoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons of cumin seeds
  • 3 dried red chillies
  • 2 large potatoes
  • 1 kg of eggplants
  • salt

Heat the oil in large, heavy-based pan over medium heat, add the cumin seeds and dried red chillies and fry for 1-2 minutes, then add the potatoes and eggplants and season with salt. Sautee for 2 minutes, then reduce the heat, cover and cook over low heat for about 10 minutes, or until the vegetables are done. Add 1-2 tablespoons of water, if the vegetables are sticking to the base of the pan.

    Khopra Paak

    Ingredients

  • 400 grams grated fresh coconut
  • 125 ml heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil or ghee
  • 300 grams sugar
  • 150 grams Khoya
  • 1 teaspoon of ground green cardamon
  • almonds

Mix the coconut and cream together ina large bowl and set aside for about 30 minutes. Brush a large baking pan lightly with oil or ghee.

Put a pan over low heat, pour in the coconut-cream mixture and cook for about 10 minutes.

Add the sugar and khoya and continue cooking over low heat for about 20 minutes.

Sprinkle over the ground cardamon, then mix well and pour into the prepared baking pan. Decorate with the almonds. Allow to cool and set, then cut into desired shapes.
